Important Instructions

Paper Specific Instructions
Duration: 180 minutes Total Marks: 100
Read the following instructions carefully

1. Go through various question palette symbols and understand their meanings before starting the examination.

2. After the start of the examination, you can view all the questions by clicking on the Question Paper button on the screen.

3. This question paper consists of 2 sections. General Aptitude (GA) for 15 marks and the subject specific section for 85 Marks. Both the sections are compulsory.

4. The GA Section consists of 10 questions. Question numbers 1 to 5 are of 4 mark each, while question numbers 6 to 10 are of 2 marks each.

5. The subject specific section consists of 55 questions, out of which question numbers 1 to 25 are of 1 mark each, while question numbers 26 to 55 are of 2 marks each.

6. Use the data given in the question while answering that question. If such data are not given, and the paper has useful data icon, then the same can be viewed by clicking on the Useful Data button that appears at the top, right-hand side of the screen.

7. The question paper consists of Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ), Multiple Select Questions (MSQ) and Numerical Answer Type (NAT).

(a) Multiple choice questions (MCQ) have four choices, out of which only ONE is the correct answer. Select the correct answer by clicking on the radio button (O) placed before the choice, with the help of the mouse.

(b) A multiple select questions (MSQ) have 4 choices, out of which ONE or MORE than ONE choice(s} is/are correct. Select the correct answers by clicking on the ratio button (O) placed before the choices, with the help of the mouse.

(c) For numerical answer type (NAT) questions, a numerical answer should be entered with the help of the mouse and the virtual numeric keypad, which will appear below the question.

8. All those questions that are not attempted will carry zero marks.

(a) Wrong answers for multiple choice type questions (MCQ) will carry NEGATIVE marks. For multiple choice type questions, a wrong answer will lead to deduction of 1/3 marks for a 1-mark question and 2/3 marks for a 2-mark question.

(b) There is NO negative marking for a wrong answer in MSQ questions. However, there is NO partial credit for choosing partially correct combinations of choices or any single wrong choice.

(c) There is no Negative marking for NAT questions.

9. Your answers will be saved on the server periodically and also at the end of the examination. The examination will step automatically at the end of 180 minutes.

10. Charts, graph sheets, calculators and mathematical tables are NOT allowed in the exam. You must use scientific calculator for calculation which is appears at the top, right-hand side of the screen.
